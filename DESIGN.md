# Casa Aura Premium Experience Template

## Overview
This website is a premium, image-led template for experience-driven local brands: spas, salons, event venues, boutique gyms, restaurants, wellness studios, private dining rooms, beauty concepts, and similar businesses where atmosphere and easy booking drive conversion.

The site should feel minimal, editorial, calm, warm, natural, aspirational, and commercially clear. It sells the feeling of being there: quality of space, care in the service, trust in the business, and convenience of reserving through WhatsApp or a direct booking action. It must feel premium without becoming stiff, and emotional without becoming playful or influencer-like.

The page is organized as eight standalone sections, with one generated design reference per section:

1. Visual powerful hero.
2. Experience/value section.
3. Featured services, packages, menu, or experiences.
4. Gallery-style visual area.
5. Social proof and reputation.
6. Location and hours.
7. FAQ or booking notes.
8. WhatsApp/reservation CTA and footer.

Every section must include one dominant image treatment. The layout should avoid repeated template blocks; the section rhythm should alternate between cinematic, editorial, practical, and conversion-focused moments while keeping the same palette, typography, button style, and image language.

## Colors
The palette is warm, natural, and premium, but it must not collapse into a flat beige-only look. Deep olive, espresso ink, burnt clay, and muted brass provide contrast and conversion strength.

- **Primary (#26352C):** Deep olive ink for primary reservation actions, navigation emphasis, dark overlays, and key conversion moments.
- **Secondary (#A55338):** Burnt clay for WhatsApp CTAs and warm booking emphasis. Use sparingly so it remains intentional.
- **Tertiary (#8B765F):** Warm stone for metadata, captions, section labels, quiet UI details, and dividers.
- **Neutral (#F7F0E6):** Warm ivory page foundation. It should feel like natural paper, plaster, or linen.
- **Surface (#FFFAF3):** Light panel surface for booking notes, FAQ rows, service details, and location information.
- **Surface Muted (#E9DDD0):** Tonal background for testimonial moments, quiet bands, and image-adjacent captions.
- **Clay Soft (#C98B69):** Supporting warmth for small rules, active states, and editorial details.
- **Olive Soft (#67735F):** Secondary natural accent for tabs, microcopy, map lines, and active gallery states.
- **Brass Soft (#B08A55):** Small premium accent for fine rules, subtle markers, and selected metadata only.
- **Ink (#1E1B17):** Main text color for readability and editorial depth.

Avoid bright blues, purple gradients, neon color, beauty-influencer pink, loud gold, glassy luxury cliches, and decorative color effects. The design should feel expensive because of proportion, image quality, and restraint.

## Typography
Use an editorial serif for emotional statements and a refined sans serif for useful interface copy. Typography should feel premium and readable, not decorative.

Recommended pairing:

- **Newsreader, Georgia, serif:** Hero headline, section titles, large testimonial quotes, and final CTA headline.
- **Inter, Arial, sans-serif:** Navigation, body text, labels, buttons, service metadata, FAQ rows, location details, and footer.

Use `display-xl` only for the hero and, when needed, the final CTA. Use `headline-lg` for major sections. Keep headings short and direct, usually one or two lines. Body copy should be concise, practical, and specific to reservation decisions: atmosphere, services, private events, hours, location, deposits, and WhatsApp flow.

Spanish CTAs should be short and commercial: "Reservar ahora", "Cotizar por WhatsApp", "Ver experiencias", "Abrir mapa", and "Hablar por WhatsApp". Do not use generic inflated copy such as "eleva tu experiencia", "transforma tu momento", or "vive lo extraordinario".

## Layout
Use a spacious editorial grid with clear image hierarchy. Desktop content should generally sit inside an 1180-1240px max-width grid with 48px page margins. Mobile should use 20px page margins and preserve the one-dominant-image-per-section rule.

The design should alternate section composition:

- **Hero:** Massive image-first opening scene with readable overlay or restrained split text. Include brand, minimal nav, headline, support copy, primary reservation CTA, and WhatsApp CTA.
- **Experience/value:** Editorial split with one large calm image and three value notes: "Ambiente cuidado", "Atencion personal", and "Reserva simple".
- **Featured experiences:** Hover-accordion style service presentation with four image slices for "Ritual de bienestar", "Cena privada", "Salon premium", and "Evento intimo".
- **Gallery:** One composed gallery block with layered image crop frames and minimal captions, not a social-media grid.
- **Social proof:** Quote-led layout with one warm lifestyle image, reputation metrics, and restrained trust details.
- **Location/hours:** Practical section with address, hours, map-like line panel, and one arrival or interior image.
- **FAQ/booking notes:** Fine-divider accordion rows with one still-life or material-detail image.
- **Final CTA:** Cinematic closing section with image overlay, WhatsApp-first conversion path, secondary reservation action, and restrained footer.

Section spacing should be generous and even. Avoid cramped blocks, repeated centered sections, card rows in every section, nested cards, and oversized decorative pills.

## Elevation & Depth
Depth should come from photography, tonal layering, editorial overlap, fine divider lines, and material contrast. Use shadows sparingly, and only as soft, broad, nearly invisible support for layered image frames or panels.

Image direction is central to the brand system. Use warm natural light, stone, wood, linen, ceramics, glass, greenery, treatment details, plated dining, intimate table settings, refined interiors, entryways, and tasteful human presence. People may be implied or shown naturally, but avoid influencer posing, stock-photo smiles, and social-media staging.

Each section should have one dominant image treatment:

- Hero: cinematic full-viewport lifestyle image.
- Experience/value: calm interior or service detail.
- Featured experiences: four slice images behaving as one accordion component.
- Gallery: one layered visual composition.
- Social proof: one guest/client atmosphere image.
- Location/hours: one arrival, facade, or interior wayfinding image.
- FAQ: one quiet still-life or tactile detail image.
- Final CTA: immersive warm hospitality or wellness scene.

Avoid glassmorphism, glowing edges, artificial depth effects, decorative orbs, bokeh blobs, random overlays, and fake app-dashboard visuals.

## Shapes
The shape language is rectilinear with subtle softness. Use 6px radius for buttons, practical panels, FAQ rows, and service controls. Use 8px radius for larger image frames, testimonial surfaces, and location panels. Use square or near-square image crops when the composition benefits from gallery rhythm.

Do not use large rounded pills as decoration. Do not over-round cards or create a playful softness. Do not put cards inside cards. Page sections should be full-width bands or open editorial layouts, with cards reserved only for repeated items, accordion rows, and practical information panels.

## Components
Buttons should make reservation feel effortless. Primary booking actions use deep olive with ivory text. WhatsApp-specific actions use burnt clay with ivory text. Secondary actions use light surfaces or text links. Every major section should include a clear next action or a nearby path toward booking, availability, WhatsApp, directions, or service detail.

Navigation is minimal: brand name, "Experiencias", "Galeria", "Ubicacion", and "FAQ". It should sit lightly over the hero or on a transparent/tonal surface. Avoid promotional badges, crowded nav items, and generic "Learn more" language.

Experience panels should work across spas, salons, restaurants, venues, boutique gyms, and wellness brands. Use the structure: name, short description, useful metadata, and one action. Metadata may include "Desde Q450", "2-8 personas", "Reserva previa", "Paquetes privados", duration, or availability.

Gallery components should feel curated and editorial. Use layered image crop frames, one strong composition, minimal captions, and fine controls. Avoid a social feed layout.

Testimonials should prioritize one credible quote, attribution, and a few reputation signals such as "4.9 promedio", "Clientes recurrentes", and "Reservas por WhatsApp". Avoid star clutter, logo walls, exaggerated claims, and influencer handles.

Location panels must be useful and elegant. Include "Zona Viva, Ciudad de Guatemala", "Mar-Sab 10:00-20:00", "Dom 10:00-16:00", a quiet map-like panel, and "Abrir mapa". Keep the information easy to scan.

FAQ accordions use hairline dividers, clear open and closed states, concise answers, and practical reservation notes. Questions should cover reservation flow, private events, inclusions, and deposits.

The final CTA should be WhatsApp-first: "Cotizar por WhatsApp" as the strongest action, with "Reservar ahora" as the secondary action. It should feel like the natural ending of the story, not a loud sales banner.

## Do's and Don'ts
- Do make atmosphere, quality, trust, and booking convenience visible from the first viewport.
- Do use one dominant image treatment per section.
- Do keep imagery warm, natural, lifestyle-oriented, and commercially polished.
- Do make WhatsApp and reservation CTAs prominent without making the brand feel casual.
- Do vary the section rhythm while keeping the same palette, typography, button style, and image language.
- Do keep Spanish copy concise, practical, and reservation-oriented.
- Do show location, hours, FAQ, and booking notes before the final CTA.
- Don't make the website feel like an influencer landing page, playful beauty account, or generic restaurant template.
- Don't use decorative orbs, bokeh blobs, purple or blue gradients, glassmorphism, sticker badges, loud review widgets, or fake dashboard graphics.
- Don't repeat the same card grid pattern across sections.
- Don't use many small unrelated thumbnails when the section calls for one dominant image treatment.
- Don't hide WhatsApp, location, hours, or reservation details only in the footer.
- Don't let the warmth become monotone beige; balance it with olive, ink, clay, brass, and strong photography.
